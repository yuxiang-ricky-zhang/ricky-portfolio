I represented ITS4US UW at the 103rd TRB Annual Meeting

× https://trb.org/AnnualMeeting/AnnualMeeting.aspx

As part of the USDOT ITS4US project, the Transportation Data Equity Initiative (TDEI) represents a collaborative effort that I am deeply involved in as a data scientist and machine learning developer at the Paul G. Allen Center for Computer Science and Engineering, University of Washington. This innovative data infrastructure initiative brings together state transportation departments, transportation service providers, industry stakeholders, and community allies. Our aim is to foster mobility equity through the development of a unified, shared data framework. This framework is designed to fill the gaps in current transportation data, ensuring reliable information availability for travelers. We focus on collecting detailed and accurate data about pedestrian networks, travel environments such as transit facilities, and on-demand travel services, which are particularly crucial for supporting the mobility needs of people with disabilities, the elderly, veterans, and residents in suburban and rural areas.

The initiative has three primary objectives: 1. Develop, refine, and expand data formats for precise mapping of pedestrian routes and transit station pathways, as well as responsive travel services. 2. Maintain and manage a comprehensive data structure for six U.S. counties over a five-year period. 3. Introduce various mobility applications and planning tools that showcase the data infrastructure's flexibility and scalability.

Innovative features of this project include the OpenSidewalks project for detailed digital mapping of pedestrian areas and the integration of GTFS-flex and GTFS-pathways data. These tools are vital for describing responsive transit services and mapping transit facilities in a digital format. This work aids in analyzing diverse mobility requirements and contributes to more inclusive urban planning.

One of the key benefits of the TDEI is the enhancement of travel experiences, especially for those with specific accessibility needs. By providing richer data on pedestrian pathways, transit routes, and on-demand transport, we aim to improve accessibility, and mobility equity, and stimulate economic activity in the involved areas. Moreover, this initiative is set to revolutionize urban planning processes by offering detailed pedestrian network data for comprehensive analysis. This data supports informed decision-making about infrastructure investments, promoting active, accessible transportation, and sustainable community development.

At the TRB 2024 conference, I had the opportunity to represent the TDEI group, showcasing our innovative data solutions and engaging with other professionals and researchers in transportation, smart city technology, and urban planning. My presentation focused on the practical applications of our machine learning models in enhancing urban mobility, and I actively participated in discussions that are now shaping our next steps in this exciting project.

I presented our transformative mapping tool at the State of the Map US 2023

× https://2023.stateofthemap.us/

Manually mapping pedestrian pathways for platforms like OpenStreetMap is challenging. It demands a vast amount of data, and the risk of error is high.

To address this, I combined existing street network data with state-of-the-art computer vision models to automatically infer pedestrian path network information for 6 cities in 2 continents. The effectiveness of this method is not just in data gathering but also in its integration and application.

Being in a standardized format, the inferred pedestrian path network data is readily usable in OpenStreetMap as a separate data layer. The standardized format also means that the data can be directly applied in real-world scenarios, leveraging the extensive reach of OpenStreetMap. Additionally, the data can be used in downstream applications, such as routing and analytics, opening doors to various urban planning and accessibility enhancement opportunities.

This tool is more than just a technical achievement; it's a step towards making cities more navigable and accessible for everyone. By bridging the gap between complex data collection and practical application, we pave the way for smarter, more inclusive urban environments. The project's scope, covering cities in various continents, highlights its potential for global impact.

I presented this project at the State of the Map US 2023, where I also connected with and learned from other mappers and developers.

I served as a panelist at the APTA Mobility Conference 2023 showcasing our innovative AI-powered solutions

× https://s6.goeshow.com/apta/mc/2023/index.cfm

Inspecting public pathways for accessibility barriers is crucial for community walkability, safety, and sustainability. Traditional inspection methods, whether conducted by professional surveyors or through crowdsourcing, are often tedious, inconsistent, costly, and difficult to scale. In contrast, modern 'smart city' initiatives focus on applying information technology to improve municipal asset management. However, sidewalks have often been overlooked in these tech-driven improvements, especially compared to roads.

Addressing this gap, we developed the Open Automated Sidewalks Inspection System (OASIS). This innovative and open-source mapping system runs on edge devices to generate sidewalk network data. OASIS utilizes advancements in neural networks, image sensing, location-based techniques, and compact hardware to segment sidewalks and identify barriers, creating a GIS layer for pedestrian transportation that is invaluable for routing, analysis, and operational reporting.

We developed a prototype of OASIS that was trained and tested in real-world settings, working alongside human surveyors from local transit pathway review teams. This prototype has demonstrated high accuracy in path mapping and significantly improved the efficiency of surveyor teams in the field. Designed with ease of adoption in mind, OASIS is built to seamlessly integrate with governmental pathway review workflows and ensure data interoperability with public data repositories.

I served as a panelist at the 2023 APTA Mobility Conference, where I showcased this innovative AI-powered solution, and had fantastic discussions with other industry experts on mobility and transportation.

Recipient of the first place Living & Inclusion Award at the Smart City Expo World Congress 2022

× AI for Inclusive Urban Sidewalks

Sidewalks are crucial for individuals with disabilities to access essential services. Yet, these urban pathways often fall short in meeting the needs of pedestrians with disabilities due to inadequate information on accessibility.

In collaboration with G3ict, I created detailed open datasets that describe the connectivity and walkability features of city sidewalks and achieved mapping of sidewalks on a large scale.

This project introduces the OpenSidewalks data standard and the AccessMap routing tool, empowering people with disabilities, civic organizations, and government agencies in 5 cities across the Americas: São Paulo, Los Angeles, Quito, Gran Valparaíso, and Santiago to make informed mobility decisions.

This project was awarded first place in the Living & Inclusion Award at the Smart City Expo World Congress 2022 .
